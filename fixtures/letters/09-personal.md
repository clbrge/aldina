June 25, 2026

Dear Theo,

I've started this letter three times now, mostly because there's so much to tell you that I don't know where to begin. So I'll just begin in the middle, the way we always used to.

The move to Lisbon finally happened. The apartment is smaller than the photos suggested — isn't it always? — but it has a little balcony that catches the morning sun, and I've already filled it with more plants than I can reasonably keep alive. From the kitchen window I can see a slice of the river, and on clear days the light comes off it in a way that stops me mid-sentence. You'd love it here. You'd also complain about the hills, constantly, and then refuse to take the tram out of principle.

Work is good — busier than I expected. I've been freelancing for two design studios, and one of them has hinted at something more permanent. I'm trying not to get ahead of myself, but it's hard not to imagine staying.

The thing I didn't expect is how much I miss our ordinary evenings. Nothing dramatic, just the long pointless conversations over bad wine. I've made a few friends here, but it takes time to build the kind of shorthand we have — the kind where you don't have to explain yourself.

Tell me everything. How's the band? Did your sister have the baby yet? Are you still pretending you're going to learn Portuguese so you can visit? (You should. I have a couch with your name on it.)

Write back soon — or better yet, book a flight.

Love always,
Mira
