To Whom It May Concern,

It is with great enthusiasm that I write in support of Ms. Clara Bennett's application to your graduate program in molecular genetics. I have known Clara for three years — first as a student in my advanced genetics seminar and later as a research assistant in my laboratory — and I can say without reservation that she is among the most capable undergraduates I have mentored in two decades of teaching.

Clara distinguished herself early. In a seminar of thirty students, she consistently asked the questions that revealed she was thinking not just about the material but about its limits: what the experiments could and could not show. When she joined my lab the following year, that same instinct made her an unusually independent researcher.

I will offer one concrete example. Midway through a project on transcription-factor binding, Clara noticed an inconsistency in our assay results that two graduate students and I had overlooked. Rather than simply flagging it, she designed and ran a control experiment over a weekend that isolated the cause to a contaminated reagent. Her diligence saved the project weeks of wasted effort and, frankly, taught the rest of us a lesson in rigor.

Beyond her technical ability, Clara is a generous colleague. She trained two newer students on our protocols and has a calm, collaborative presence that strengthens the whole lab. She is exactly the kind of researcher who makes those around her better.

I recommend Clara to you in the strongest possible terms. She has the intellect, the work ethic, and the temperament to thrive in a demanding doctoral program, and I am confident she will make significant contributions to her field. Please do not hesitate to contact me if I can provide any further information.

Sincerely,
Dr. Helena Marsh
Professor of Molecular Biology
